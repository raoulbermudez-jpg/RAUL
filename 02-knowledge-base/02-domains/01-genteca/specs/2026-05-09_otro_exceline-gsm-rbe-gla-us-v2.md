# Exceline Voltage Protector for Air Conditioners and Refrigerators

## Product Overview

The GSM-RBE is an electronic voltage protector designed to protect air conditioners, refrigerators, and motors against voltage failures caused by blackouts, voltage rises, and voltage drops.

## Features

1. Normal Voltage and Delay Cycle indicator light
2. High Voltage indicator light
3. Low Voltage indicator light
4. High Voltage Adjustment
5. Low Voltage Adjustment
6. ON/OFF switch
7. Intelligent Delay Cycle adjustment between 180 – 300 seconds
8. Holes for wall installation or 2x4 electrical box mounting
9. Two convenient wire access points: one for electrical box and one by the side through removable sections for wall installations
10. Terminal block for secure connections
11. Safety cover with sealing holes to avoid manipulation and electrical risk

## Operating Principles

**HIGH VOLTAGE DETECTION:**
If the voltage exceeds the maximum value selected, the red light indicator of the High Voltage Indicator will turn on and the protected equipment will turn off.

**LOW VOLTAGE DETECTION:**
If the voltage is below the minimum value selected, the red light indicator of the Low Voltage Indicator will turn on and the protected equipment will turn off.

**NORMAL OPERATION:**
When voltage returns to normal range, any indication of failure will disappear. A green blinking light will turn on and start the Time Delay Cycle. Once the adjusted time of the Delay Cycle is over, the green light will stop blinking and the equipment will turn on, ensuring the voltage is within the normal range. The protector features an intelligent timer that avoids unnecessary waiting time and ensures proper connection of equipment.

## Installation Instructions

1. **Disconnect the power supply.**

2. **Mounting Options:**
   - If installing in a 2x4 electrical box, screw it directly
   - If installing on wall, use the Drilling Guide included in the package and screw the protector into the surface (screws not included)

3. **Preparation:**
   - Make sure the switch is in the "0" (off) position
   - Strip about 3/8 inch of insulation from each wire
   - For loads greater than 20 amps, use a circuit breaker and contactor as shown in the connection diagram

## Wiring Instructions

Before making connections to the protector, verify that all cables are properly identified, including those of the equipment to be protected and those of the power supply.

**Terminal Block Connections:**
- **L** = Line Input (120V model)
- **L1** = Line 1 Input (220V model)
- **L2** = Line 2 Input/output (220V model)
- **G** = Ground
- **N** = Neutral
- **S** = Line output (120V model)
- **S** = Line1 output (220V model)

To ensure good connections:
- Verify each electrical wire is connected to the right terminal block
- Use the appropriate tool to tighten cables
- Maximum tightening torque: 0.5 N*M (5.0 KG*CM)
- Tighten the screw clockwise

**Cable Requirements:**
- Use the appropriate cable for the consumption of your equipment
- Maximum cable size: AWG 10
- Recommended fixing screws: No. 6
