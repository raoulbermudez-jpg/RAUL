# GSPTMV Submersible Pump Integral Protection Relay

## 1. General Description

GSPTMV is an electronic Submersible Pump Integral Protection Relay that constantly supervises the Motor Current and the main electrical parameters such as:
- Voltage
- Frequency
- Power factor
- Real power
- Reactive power
- Energy consumption

The relay provides the most reliable protection against:
- Overload
- Phase failure
- Phase reversal
- Single phasing
- Unbalanced conditions
- Overtemperature
- Excessive starts per hour

### Warnings and Cautions

**WARNING:** Only qualified technicians with knowledge about protection relays and associated machinery should do the installation, starting up, and maintenance of the system. Failure to comply may result in equipment damage and/or personal injury.

**WARNING:** This product may start automatically. The user must take cautions to avoid hazards to people.

**CAUTION:** An incorrectly applied or installed GSPTMV can result in damage to the components or reduction in product life. Wiring or application errors, or operating/storing in excessive ambient temperatures may result in malfunction of the GSPTMV.

**CAUTION:** This product has been designed for industrial environment. Use of this product in residential environment may cause unwanted electromagnetic disturbances, in which case the user may be required to take adequate mitigation measures.

## 2. Parts and Pieces

### GSPTMV Detachable CT Box

**CAUTION:** Each set of GSPTMV and attachable CT box is calibrated and have the same Factory Number. Before assembling the CT box verify that its factory number is the same as the GSPTMV one. Failure to comply may result in a bigger measure error.

## 3. GSPTMV - CT Box Assembly and Disassembly

### 3.1 Instructions for GSPTMV - CT Box Assembly

a) Move the supporting brackets 2mm outside and push down GSPTMV to CT Box until back groove of relay can insert into CT Box guide rail, using Alignment Mark as reference.

b) From Alignment Mark, move GSPTMV relay towards the left sliding through rail guide until it clicks.

c) Insert both CT Input Terminals connecting GSPTMV and CT Box.

### 3.2 Instructions for GSPTMV - CT Box Dismounting

a) Disconnect both CT Input Terminals used for GSPTMV and CT Box connection and pull down supporting brackets by means of screwdrivers.

b) Move GSPTMV towards the right sliding through CT Box guide rail until it gets to alignment mark.

c) Pull out GSPTMV upwards from CT Box.

## 4. GSPTMV DIN Rail Mounting

**CAUTION:** GSPTMV must be installed in an accessible position free from dust, dirt, dampness and vibration. Allow enough space for air circulation around the enclosure and easy access to all operator controls. Indoors use only.

### 4.1 Instructions for Mechanical Installation with CT Box

a) Place GSPTMV at inclined position and its back side must be placed toward the upper edge of the DIN Rail and then push down GSPTMV relay as shown in figure until it clicks on the rail.

### 4.2 Instructions for Mechanical Installation with CT Box

Once you have assembled CT Box on GSPTMV (See item 3.1) you shall follow these mounting instructions:

a) Place GSPTMV at inclined position so that its back side (CT Box) must be placed toward the upper edge of the DIN Rail and then push down GSPTMV relay as shown in figure until it clicks on the rail.

## 5. GSPTMV Flat Surface Mounting

### 5.1 Instructions for Mechanical Installation without CT Box

a) Take off the four (4) mounting ears at back side of GSPTMV, insert and slip both mounting ears into the GSPTMV back side grooves.

b) Place GSPTMV over flat surface panel and fix it handling a screwdriver suitable for screws 3/16" x 1/2".

**Recommendations for Flat Surface Mounting:**
Make four (4) holes (5/32") on panel surface before installing. See as reference The Guide for Flat Surface mounting as shown at Item 7.2 (Dimensions GSPTMV without Detachable CT Box).

### 5.2 Instructions for Mechanical Installation with CT Box

Once you have assembled Detachable CT Box on GSPTMV (Item 3.1):

a) Take off the four (4) mounting ears at back side of CT Box. Insert and slip both mounting ears into the CT Box back side grooves.

b) Place GSPTMV with detachable CT Box over flat surface panel and fix it handling a screwdriver suitable for screws 3/16" x 1/2".

**Recommendations for Flat Surface Mounting:**
Make four (4) holes (5/32") on panel surface before installing. See as reference The Guide for Flat Surface Mounting shown at Item 7.1 (Dimensions GSPTMV with Detachable CT Box).

**WARNING:** Disconnect power supply before installing GSPTMV. Failure to comply may result in death or serious injury.

**CAUTION:** Check that the voltage and current of chosen GSPTMV model corresponds to line voltage and motor current.

## 6. GSPTMV General Dimensions

Dimensions available for GSPTMV with and without Detachable CT Box in open and closed configurations.

## 7. GSPTMV Operation

GSPTMV constantly supervises current and voltage values; when any harmful condition occurs, the output connection is deactivated until the fault disappears and power line conditions return to an acceptable level.

Specific timing such as Start Up Delay and Fault Detection Delay are incorporated to prevent nuisance tripping due to rapid power fluctuations.

GSPTMV provides LCD Display to indicate the output status including:
- Voltage
- Current
- Unbalance
- Frequency
- Load status

The relay also provides four (4) push buttons (On/Off, Up, Down and Select) for electrical parameter adjustment such as:
- Voltage
- Current
- Frequency
- Fault Detection Delay
- Other parameters
