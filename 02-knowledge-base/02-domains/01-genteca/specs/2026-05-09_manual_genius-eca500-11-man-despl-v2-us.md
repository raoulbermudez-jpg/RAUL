# ECA500-11 Total Motor Protection Relay

## General Description

The ECA500-11 is an electronic Total Motor Protection Relay that constantly supervises Motor Current and main electrical parameters including:
- Voltage
- Power Factor
- Real power
- Reactive power
- Energy consumption

The relay provides reliable protection against:
- Overload
- Phase failure
- Phase reversal
- Single phasing
- Unbalanced conditions

## Safety Warnings

**WARNING:** This product may start automatically. The user must take precautions to avoid hazards to people.

**WARNING:** Disconnect power supply before installing the ECA500-11. Failure to comply may result in death or serious injury.

**CAUTION:** An incorrectly applied or installed ECA500-11 can result in damage to components or reduction in product life. Wiring or application errors, or operating/storing it in excessive ambient temperatures may result in malfunction.

**CAUTION:** Check that the voltage and current chosen for the ECA500-11 model corresponds to line voltage and motor current.

## Mechanical Installation Instructions

### Installation without CT Box

a) Place ECA500-11 at an inclined position so that its back side (CT Box) is placed toward the upper edge of the DIN Rail.

b) Push the relay downward until it does CLICK on the DIN Rail.

### CT Box Assembly

When assembling the CT box, verify that its factory number is the same as the ECA500-11 one. Failure to comply may result in a bigger measurement error.

**CAUTION:** Each CT Box has to have the same Factory calibration data number as the ECA500-11 before the CT box is assembled. Ensure the CT Box is CLOSED after assembly.

## Wiring Recommendations

- Avoid over-tightening M 2.5 screws upon terminals during wiring connection. Torque max: 4.5 lb-in (5.18 kgf-cm).
- Terminals wiring size: Between 12 AWG and 18 AWG.
- Wire Strip Length: 7–8 mm.
- Maximum diameter of CT Box Holes wiring size: AWG 0 (18 mm).
- Connect L1, L2, L3 terminals for Voltage Input in parallel connection before line starter circuit through Contactor (as shown in Diagram Connection).
- Use three CT holes for pass wiring to 3-phase motor. Using less than three wires may cause undesired current unbalance.
